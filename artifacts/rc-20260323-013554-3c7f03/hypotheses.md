# Research Hypotheses

Based on the literature synthesis, the following hypotheses guided this systematic review:

## H1: Communication Quality
Patient-provider racial concordance is associated with improved communication quality, including longer visit duration, more patient-centered communication, and greater shared decision-making for Black patients.

## H2: Patient Satisfaction and Trust
Racial concordance is associated with significantly higher patient satisfaction ratings and greater trust in the healthcare provider among Black patients, partially mediated by reduced medical mistrust.

## H3: Treatment Adherence
Racial concordance is associated with improved treatment adherence for Black patients, with effect size varying by disease category and treatment complexity.

## H4: Health Outcomes
Racial concordance is associated with improved health outcomes for Black patients, including reduced mortality and increased preventive service utilization, with effects concentrated in high-complexity clinical scenarios.

## H5: Mechanism
The effects of racial concordance on healthcare quality operate primarily through four mechanisms: implicit bias reduction, enhanced trust, communication concordance, and patient activation.
