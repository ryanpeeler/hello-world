## Key Findings for Future Runs

### Conceptual
- Cultural fluency is best understood as a synthesis of individual competence/humility and structural competency frameworks
- The individual-structural tension is the generative core of the analysis
- "Infrastructure" framing shifts the policy conversation from training budgets to institutional design

### Empirical
- Three systematic reviews (Beach 2005, Lie 2011, Truong 2014) establish the training ceiling effect
- Alsan et al. (2019) Oakland RCT is the strongest causal evidence for concordance/fluency effects
- Greenwood et al. (2020) newborn mortality data provides the most dramatic outcome disparity
- Hoffman et al. (2016) 50% false belief rate is the most cited implicit bias finding

### Gaps Identified
- No RCT testing integrated cultural fluency infrastructure interventions
- Limited cost-effectiveness data for structural vs. individual approaches
- Telehealth and cultural fluency: emerging and under-studied
- Intersectionality (race x gender x SES x age) largely unaddressed in training literature
