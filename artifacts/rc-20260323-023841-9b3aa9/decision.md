# Research Decision

## Decision: Proceed to Paper Drafting

Based on the literature synthesis, the evidence base is sufficient to support a comprehensive literature review arguing for cultural fluency as structural clinical infrastructure. The key analytical contribution — the tension between individual training and structural intervention — is well-supported by the reviewed evidence.

## Rationale

1. **Sufficient evidence base**: 38 verified sources spanning all five target domains provide adequate coverage for a literature review.
2. **Clear conceptual contribution**: The "cultural fluency as infrastructure" framing synthesizes the individual (cultural competence/humility) and structural (structural competency) literatures in a novel way.
3. **Central tension is empirically grounded**: The weak patient-outcome effects of individual training (Beach 2005; Lie 2011; Truong 2014) and the structural barriers documented by Bach (2004) and Metzl & Hansen (2014) establish both sides of the individual-structural tension.
4. **Policy relevance**: The framework has direct implications for medical education reform, institutional policy, and health equity initiatives.

## Key Risks

- The term "cultural fluency" is not yet standardized in the literature; the paper must carefully distinguish it from existing constructs.
- Experimental evidence directly testing cultural fluency as infrastructure (rather than training) is limited.
- The simulated experiment provides illustrative rather than confirmatory evidence.
