# Research Goal

**Topic:** Beyond cultural competency training: cultural fluency as structural clinical infrastructure and its effects on diagnostic accuracy, patient trust, treatment adherence, and health disparities in Black and minority patient populations in the United States.

## Research Questions

1. How does cultural fluency differ from cultural competency as a conceptual framework, and what are the implications of this distinction for clinical practice?
2. What is the evidence that cultural fluency — operationalized as structural clinical infrastructure rather than individual provider skill — affects diagnostic accuracy in cross-racial clinical encounters?
3. How does cultural fluency at both individual and institutional levels influence patient trust and therapeutic alliance, particularly among Black patients with documented medical mistrust?
4. What are the mechanisms through which cultural fluency infrastructure affects treatment adherence and shared decision-making?
5. To what extent can cultural fluency, deployed as institutional infrastructure rather than individual training, reduce health disparities for Black and minority populations?
6. **Central tension:** What is the relationship between individual provider cultural fluency training and structural/systems-level interventions, and can either approach succeed in isolation?
