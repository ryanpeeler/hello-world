# Research Hypotheses

## H1: Cultural Fluency as Infrastructure Hypothesis
Cultural fluency, when operationalized as structural clinical infrastructure (embedded in institutional workflows, hiring practices, decision support systems, and organizational culture) rather than as individual provider training alone, produces larger and more durable reductions in health disparities for Black and minority patients than either approach in isolation.

## H2: Trust Mediation Hypothesis
The effect of cultural fluency on health outcomes is primarily mediated by patient trust, which operates at two distinct levels: interpersonal trust in the provider (influenced by individual fluency) and institutional trust in the healthcare system (influenced by structural features). Both trust pathways must be activated for optimal outcomes.

## H3: Training Ceiling Hypothesis
Individual cultural competency/fluency training exhibits diminishing returns above a threshold of investment, with remaining disparities attributable to structural factors that training cannot address. This ceiling effect explains the weak patient-outcome findings in systematic reviews of training interventions (Beach et al. 2005; Lie et al. 2011; Truong et al. 2014).

## H4: Concordance Substitution Hypothesis
Cultural fluency can partially substitute for racial concordance in producing the trust, communication quality, and diagnostic accuracy benefits associated with concordance. However, the substitution is incomplete: some concordance effects (shared lived experience, identity-based trust) cannot be fully replicated through fluency alone, establishing a complementary rather than substitutive relationship.

## H5: Diagnostic Accuracy Pathway
Cultural fluency infrastructure reduces diagnostic disparities through two mechanisms: (a) individual bias mitigation (reducing differential interpretation of symptoms) and (b) structural supports (clinical decision tools, diverse team composition) that create redundancy against individual bias failures.
