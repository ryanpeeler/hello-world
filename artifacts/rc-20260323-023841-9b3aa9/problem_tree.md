# Problem Decomposition — Cultural Fluency as Clinical Infrastructure

## Root Problem
Health disparities persist for Black and minority patients in the United States despite decades of cultural competency training initiatives. The dominant paradigm treats cultural competency as an individual provider skill rather than as structural clinical infrastructure, limiting its effectiveness.

## Branch 1: Definitional and Conceptual Gaps
- Cultural competence (Cross 1989) → cultural humility (Tervalon & Murray-García 1998) → structural competency (Metzl & Hansen 2014) → cultural fluency as infrastructure (this review)
- The field lacks a unified operational definition of "cultural fluency" that distinguishes it from prior constructs
- Measurement instruments remain anchored to individual competency rather than institutional capacity

## Branch 2: Diagnostic Accuracy
- Implicit bias affects clinical decision-making (Hoffman et al. 2016; Hall et al. 2015)
- Racial disparities in pain assessment, cardiovascular diagnosis, maternal health screening
- Individual bias mitigation training shows limited durability (Penner et al. 2010)
- Structural interventions (decision support, diverse teams) show more promise

## Branch 3: Patient Trust
- Historical mistrust rooted in Tuskegee and ongoing structural racism (Gamble 1997)
- Trust mediates healthcare utilization and disclosure (Boulware et al. 2003)
- Concordance increases trust but is not scalable as sole intervention (Alsan et al. 2019)
- Cultural fluency may rebuild trust independent of concordance

## Branch 4: Treatment Adherence
- Racial gaps in medication adherence (Traylor et al. 2010)
- Shared decision-making improves adherence but requires cultural fluency (Charles et al. 1997)
- Language concordance vs. cultural fluency — distinct mechanisms

## Branch 5: Structural vs. Individual Tension (Central)
- Training-only approaches show weak evidence (Beach et al. 2005; Lie et al. 2011; Truong et al. 2014)
- Structural competency calls for systems-level change (Metzl & Hansen 2014)
- Neither approach sufficient in isolation
- "Cultural fluency as infrastructure" = the integration thesis

## Branch 6: Medical Education Pipeline
- Current curricula emphasize cultural competency as discrete module (Jernigan et al. 2016; Dogra et al. 2009)
- Need for structural embedding across curriculum (Kumagai 2009; Neff et al. 2020)
- Workforce diversity as complementary structural intervention (Saha 2008)
